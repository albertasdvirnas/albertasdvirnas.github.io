About me

Background & Research

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Mathematics and Mathematical Statistics, Umeå University. My work sits at the intersection of applied mathematics, statistical modelling, and data-driven science — developing new methods for time series analysis and applying them across disciplines.

My research focuses on building and adapting time series analysis methods that can bridge the gap between mathematics and real scientific questions. This cross-disciplinary approach has led me to work on problems in biophysics — such as single-molecule imaging and optical DNA mapping — as well as in ecology and other fields where complex temporal data demands rigorous mathematical treatment.

I completed my PhD at Lund University (2022) under the supervision of Prof. Tobias Ambjörnsson in the Computational Biology and Biological Physics group, where I developed barcode analysis algorithms for optical DNA mapping. During my PhD I also worked as an in-lab theoretician in Fredrik Westerlund's lab at Chalmers University of Technology.
