About me

Background & Research

I am a researcher working at the intersection of biophysics, computational data analysis, and single-molecule imaging. My work focuses on developing algorithms and software tools that extract meaningful biological information from complex optical measurements.

My primary research area is Optical DNA Mapping (ODM) — a technique that stretches individual DNA molecules in nanochannels and images sequence-specific fluorescent patterns, enabling fast genome assembly and structural variant detection without full sequencing.

I am passionate about bridging the gap between wet-lab experiments and rigorous mathematical and computational analysis — turning raw microscopy images into biological insight.
